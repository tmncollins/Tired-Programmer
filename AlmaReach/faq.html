Data Enrichment

We only use publicly information that could be accessed by a manual researcher. This includes public webpages and search results, national registries such as Companies House and the Charity Commission, and official professional registries.

We never access private databases, restricted systems, or credentials-protect content. We do not use any data that is not publicly available, and we do not use any data that would require special access or permissions to obtain.

We provide a full profile for all alumni, including all relevant information found on:

  • Current employment information
  • Career history
  • Company directorships and charity trusteeships
  • Honours and titles
  • Notable achievements
  • Due diligence risks
We then use propriety models to provide estimates of income, wealth and giving capacity, as well as an estimate of likely career seniority and progression over the medium-term.

We also offer a range of other services, such as bespoke analytics, mentoring allocation, and deep due diligence research.

To us, data enrichment involves supplementing existing alumni records with information which will inform and assist Development Offices in a range of activities. We believe in making life as easy as possible for fundraising campaigns and alumni relations.

This involves several stages:

  1. We start with a basic alumni record provided by the institution, including such info as name, subject, graduation year, etc.
  2. We then use this information to search for relevant data across a wide range of public sources.
  3. A custom identity resolution engine is applied to ensure any information kept refers to the alum in question.
  4. Finally, we distill the key points of the information retrieved to and compile a full profile, along with a bibliography of data sources used.

Yes. We have carefully designed our data retrieval and enrichment process to ensure the highest possible standard of accuracy and relevance, whilst still providing comprehensive profiles. This involves cross-referencing key facts from a diverse array of sources, where available.

We also include a bibliography of sources used for each profile, so institutions can verify the information provided if desired.

We have also carried out a series of tests alongside manual research, to check for false positives and negatives, finding a high level of sensitivity and specificity in our output. For further details of precise test results, please do get in touch.
